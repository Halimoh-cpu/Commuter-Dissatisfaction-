# Problem Statement

### Urban commuters in Lagos face a daily transportation crisis that is eroding their confidence, productivity, and quality of life. Despite the existence of multiple public and private transit options; including BRT, Keke, Ride-Hailing, and Shuttle services, commuters continue to experience persistent delays, safety concerns, high fares, and service unreliability that leave the majority deeply dissatisfied with their transit experience.

### MooveSmarts Transits operates across key Lagos routes but lacks a clear, data-driven understanding of which specific routes, transport modes, passenger categories, weather conditions, and complaint types are most responsible for driving commuter dissatisfaction. Without this understanding, the company cannot strategically allocate resources, prioritise service improvements, or design targeted interventions that address the root causes of the dissatisfaction crisis rather than its surface symptoms.

### This project directly addresses that gap. Using data from 4,500 trips across multiple routes, transport modes, and passenger categories, this dashboard identifies the specific dimensions of commuter dissatisfaction,  revealing that 62% of commuters are dissatisfied, average delays reach 13.17 minutes, and traffic delay accounts for 32.06% of all complaints, and delivers a prioritised, evidence-based set of recommendations to help MooveSmarts Transits improve service quality, reduce commuter dissatisfaction, and build a more reliable and trusted urban transit system in Lagos.

#Key Findings
##	Finding	Key Number
### 1	Overall commuter dissatisfaction rate	62%
### 2	Most delayed route	Ikorodu-CMS (28.09%)
### 3	Second most delayed route	Berger-CMS (27.74%)
### 4	Top two routes share of all delays	55.83% combined
### 5	Dissatisfaction spread across transport modes	Nearly equal — max gap 2.14%
### 6	Most delay-affected passenger category	Visitors (27.03%)
### 7	Top complaint type	Traffic Delay (32.06%)
### 8	Delay and traffic complaints combined	59.30% of all complaints
### 9	Delays during rainy weather	55.83%
### 10	Driver rating range	3.31 to 3.37 — all below satisfactory

 # Recommendations
### 1 Prioritise congestion management on Ikorodu-CMS and Berger-CMS routes
### 2 Implement system-wide service quality improvement across all transport modes
### 3 Develop visitor-specific transit support and information system
### 4 Launch a dedicated traffic delay mitigation strategy
### 5 Develop rainy season operational protocols and weather-ready vehicle deployment
### 6 Invest in driver training and real-time passenger feedback system
### 7 Address cost transparency and conduct vehicle safety audits proactively

# Conclusion
### A 62% dissatisfaction rate across 4,500 trips confirms a systemic transit quality crisis in Lagos. Delays on Ikorodu-CMS and Berger-CMS routes alone account for over half of all delay incidents. Rainy weather amplifies the problem significantly. Addressing these findings requires simultaneous action across route management, weather preparedness, driver quality, and fare transparency — with commuter satisfaction improvement from 38% to a minimum of 70% as the strategic target.
